Hello and welcome to this second blog post on this website. This post is going to be about ethical hacking
and my view on it. Sit back and enjoy this rant.


For you to be able to understand this you first have to understand what ethical hacking is. Ethical hacking
is when a company pays an individual or comapny to try to break into their company and gain sensitive info
from it. Most ethical hackers go solo and ofering freelance work but there are a few companies which offer
this as a service.


From my own knowledge most ethical hackers prefer to either use kali linux or parrot OS due to their high amounts
of preinstalled tools and privacy and versitility it offers (As well as literally daily updates). Since I don't have
another physical machine to run it on, I just ran it as a 1 core, 8GB RAM machine running of my 1TB USB HDD. As you
can imagine it was pretty painful(It took 3 hours to install nessus.) to use with it just having one core but apart from that
it was pretty fast to use. I then used TryHackMe to learn about the various tools that were on the system. I learned how to
effectivly use nmap, metasploit and hashcat (Among other things). There is a lot I have learned from this experience
and I have a new found knowledge of nmap and hashcat and Burp Suite and tools like that. Maybe another day I might
go back to doing ethical hacking and finding vulnerabilities but for now I'm doing nothing and just cementing my
knowledge of the subject.


Thanks for reading this rant and about my ethical hacking journey.
